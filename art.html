Lobster Telephone

Date: 1938
Medium: Steel, plaster, rubber, resin and paper
Dimensions: 178 × 330 × 178 mm
Original Title: Téléphone - Homard
Description: Lobsters and telephones are strong sexual connotations for Dalí himself. The telephone often appears in certain paintings of the late 1930s such as Mountain Lake, and the lobster appears in drawings and designs, usually associated with erotic pleasure and pain. In Lobster Telephone, the lobster's tail, where its sexual parts are located, is placed directly over the mouthpiece of the telephone.

Portrait of Mrs. Isabel Styler-Tas

Date: 1945
Technique: Oil on canvas
Dimensions: 65.5 x 86cm
Signature: Signed and dated lower right: Salvador Dalí 1945
Location: Staatliche Museen zu Berlin, Nationalgalerie, Berlin
Description: This work, painted in Hollywood, owes less to Dali's own past experiments with double images than to the work of the sixteenth-century Italian artist Giuseppe Arcimboldo (1527-93), who was generally admired by Surrealism enthusiasts. Arcimboldo's paintings, which often depicts human figures created out of twigs, leaves and fruit, bear a very close resemblance to the structure on the left of Dali's painting, which might be where Dali got his inspirations for this work from.
Attribution:Richard Mortel from Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, CC BY 2.0, via Wikimedia Commons

Gala at the Window Statue

Date: 1973/76
Technique: Modelled Wax
Dimensions: 23.5 cm (9.2 in)
Original Title: Original title: Gala asomada a la ventana (aka «Gala a la ventana»)
Description: Gala Diaconovna-Dalí is leaning out of a window. She looks out on to the dream, on to the fantasy of her childhood in St. Petersburg, the Northern capital of the Czars, the French and Italian city which Peter the Great wanted to open to the Baltic and to Europe.This piece, shaped like an “A”, was part of Dali’s project to create the name GALA. His goals – which he never fulfilled – was to complete the set with two more sculptures in the shape of “G” and “L”.

The Nobility of Time

Date: 1984
Technique: Modelled Wax
Dimensions: 16-feet tall (5 meters)
Original Title: Original title: “La Noblesse du Temps”
Location: Plaça de la Rotonda,Andorra Vella
Description: The nobility of time is a monumental bronze sculpture, which is weighing 1,400 kilograms and is rising up to 4.90 meters above the ground. It's made by Salvador Dali, and it symbolizes the passing time,and how it dominates over humanity as a whole. There are quite a lot of art enthusiasts across the globe, who are eager to visit Andorra just to enjoy this authentic piece of genius.
Attribution: From needpix.com.

The Rainbow

Date: 1972
Technique: Painted Plaster Relief
Dimensions: 29.2 x 47.6 x 2.6 cm
Description: The overall content of The Rainbow probably deals with the mother-son relationship and according to historical documents, a fact that Dali affronted his mother's name and was disowned by his father. The clues to decipher the work's message of the images are clear: observe a woman brandishes a hay fork while holding a child's hand as they run towards a rainbow emerging from a cataclysmic, rock-strewn, horizon mounted by three figures.
Attribution: Salvador Dalí, Public domain, via Wikimedia Commons

Dali Atomicus

Date: 1941
Technique: Photograph
Description: The 1948 work Dali Atomicus explores the idea of suspension(in midair), depicting three cats flying, water thrown from a bucket, an easel, a footstool and Salvador Dalí all in mid-air. The title of the photograph is a reference to Dalí's work Leda Atomica (at that which can be seen in the right of the photograph behind the two cats.)
Attribution: Philippe Halsman, Public domain, via Wikimedia Commons
